### The Confidence Sequence

The brand's signature rhetorical move:

1. Make a strong claim.
2. Immediately name a limit, failure, or gap.
3. Reframe that admission as the actual source of credibility.

**Example:** "Accordingly, we are wrong, and we accept that. But we will evaluate the long-term success of this process on how well we accumulate cumulative knowledge through it in a way that makes us less wrong."

### Humor Mechanisms (in order of preference)

1. **Unexpected analogy** — capital-M Market insight from an unrelated domain (antique dolls, composting, a 1970s flea market)
2. **Tonal pivot** — earnest setup, then one-sentence blunt punchline. "It ruled."
3. **Category violation** — treating something finance takes seriously as obviously absurd, or vice versa

Mild profanity ("bullshit detector," "dreck, dross, and douchebags") is a seasoning, not the dish. Humor is absent from compliance, factsheets, policy.

### Crisis Communications

1. State the bad news first. No throat-clearing.
2. Name what happened, not what it means for you.
3. Use "I" not "we." Bad news lands better from a person.
4. Apply the confidence sequence: honest assessment → name the limit → explain what changes.
5. Never promise recovery.

**Don't write:** "We are monitoring the situation carefully and remain committed to our process."
**Write:** "Here's what happened. Here's what we missed. Here's what it means for the position going forward."

## Client Email Voice

### Openings

- **Established client:** Name + em dash ("Amethyst —", "Thorn —")
- **Quick reply:** No salutation ("This is definitely the right stuff!")
- **Never:** "Dear [Name]," or "I hope this email finds you well."

### Sign-off (canonical)

> Sending good vibes your way from Utah —
>
> Sloane

Followed by formal signature block, even on one-sentence emails. The contrast is intentional.

### Operational instructions

1. Confirm their situation in their terms
2. Give a realistic time estimate
3. State next steps clearly, sequenced
4. Pre-empt the likely snag
5. Invite follow-up casually: "lmk if any questions come up"

### Errors

Own mistakes without excessive apology. Direct correction, no elaboration.

**Don't:** "I'm so sorry for the confusion this may have caused."
**Do:** "One other note: I misspoke about the other account — it's a regular investing account, not a HY savings account."

### AI Transparency

Never hide AI assistance. Frame warmly: "Our friendly local robot pulled together these instructions for you."

| Prohibited                                 | Use instead                                          |
| ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| "ESG" (as self-descriptor)                 | "ethical," "values-aligned," "our screening process" |
| "ESG investing/screening/ratings/score"    | Equivalent ethical/values-aligned terms              |
| "Socially responsible investing" / "SRI"   | "ethical investing"                                  |
| "Sustainable investing" (as primary label) | "ethical investing"                                  |
| "Impact investing"                         | "ethical investing"                                  |
| "SEC-registered"                           | "state-registered investment adviser (Utah)"         |
| "CFA charterholder" re: Sloane             | "former CFA Institute Content Manager"               |
| "we believe" in formal copy                | Direct assertion                                     |

**Exceptions:**
- Using "ESG" to *critique* the ESG industry is brand-compliant. "ESG" as external critique = OK.
- Using "ESG" when *comparing ourselves to* the ESG industry is brand-compliant. Clients expect us to position against ESG — saying "we don't use third-party ESG ratings" or "we do not rely on ESG scores" is a comparison to an external standard, not a self-descriptor.
- "ESG" as self-descriptor = never.

### Absolute prohibited terms (compliance, strict liability)

**Guarantees:** guaranteed, risk-free, safe (absolute), secure (absolute), insured (unless FDIC/SIPC), protected (unless contractual)

**Superlatives:** best, top, #1, leading, premier, elite, superior, unmatched, world-class

**Performance absolutes:** proven, will outperform, beats the market, always outperforms, never loses, 100%, certain, definite

**Ethical absolutes:** fully ethical, zero-harm, conflict-free, 100% ethical, completely values-aligned, pure

## Compliance Essentials

Primary regulation: SEC Rule 206(4)-1 (Marketing Rule). This is a first-pass screen, not legal advice.

### Seven bright lines

1. **No backtested/hypothetical performance on public pages.** Internal only.
2. **No gross performance without net.** Net = gross minus 1% fee. Equal prominence, same period.
3. **No invented statistics.** Verify AUM, client count, exclusion percentages before every use.
4. **Media citations must include year.**
5. **Media coverage is not endorsement.** "Quoted in" or "Profiled in" — never "Endorsed by."
6. **No "refuse all conflicts of interest."** Form ADV discloses conflicts; claiming elimination is a violation.
7. **Absolute prohibited terms** (see above).

### Pre-approved phrasing (use verbatim)

| Context            | Approved phrasing                                                                                                                    |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Performance        | "We aim to deliver strong risk-adjusted returns consistent with our clients' values."                                                |
| Performance        | "Our goal is to construct portfolios that outperform over full market cycles."                                                       |
| Screening limits   | "Our screening process has limitations. We acknowledge that screening is imperfect and that our understanding of companies evolves." |
| Concentration risk | "Our concentrated approach means individual position outcomes have a meaningful impact on overall portfolio performance."            |
| Process humility   | "We are wrong, and we accept that."                                                                                                  |
| Ethics framing     | "Most investors treat ethics as a constraint. We understand them as insights."                                                       |
| Registration       | "State-registered investment adviser (Utah, CRD #316032)"                                                                            |
| Sloane credentials | "former CFA Institute Content Manager"                                                                                               |
| Media citations    | "Quoted in [Publication] ([Year])"                                                                                                   |
| Conflicts          | "We manage conflicts of interest as disclosed in our Form ADV Part 2A."                                                              |

### Required disclosures

**Any performance reference:**
*Past performance does not guarantee future results. All investing involves risk, including possible loss of principal. Performance figures reflect a 1% annual advisory fee.*

**Any reference to 15-25 concentrated holdings:**
*Concentrated portfolios involve higher risk than diversified portfolios. Individual security performance has a meaningful impact on overall portfolio results.*

**Any screening capability claim:**
*Our screening process has limitations and may not identify all companies that conflict with client values.*

### Compliance pre-flight checklist

- [ ] No absolute prohibited terms
- [ ] Any performance claim uses approved phrasing with required disclosures
- [ ] Media mentions include year and use "Quoted in" / "Profiled in"
- [ ] Concentration risk disclosure accompanies any 15-25 holdings reference
- [ ] Screening limitation disclosure accompanies any screening capability claim
- [ ] No backtested or hypothetical performance on public pages
- [ ] Consistent with Form ADV Part 2A
- [ ] All quantitative claims are footnoted with primary sources
