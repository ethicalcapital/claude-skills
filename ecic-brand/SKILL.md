# ECIC Brand Voice Skill

This skill contains the complete brand, voice, compliance, and visual identity guide for Ethical Capital
Investment Collaborative. All brand knowledge lives in `references/brand-guidelines.md`. Load it at the
start of any content task.

## When to use this skill

- **Creating content**: emails, research articles, website copy, factsheets, social posts, pitch decks,
  adviser materials, institutional letters
- **Reviewing content**: checking anything written for ECIC against voice, messaging, and compliance rules
- **Brand questions**: tone choices, prohibited terms, I vs. We, audience targeting
- **Visual work**: color, typography, gradients, logo specs

## Workflow

### 1. Load brand guidelines

Read `references/brand-guidelines.md` at the start of every task. It is the single source of truth.

### 2. Identify content type and audience

Before writing or reviewing, determine:
- **Content type**: use the Tone by Context matrix in the guidelines
- **Target audience**: individual investor, return skeptic, RIA, institution, or general (see Audience Profiles)
- **I vs. We**: follow the I/We rules in the guidelines exactly

### 3. Apply voice constants

These never change regardless of content type:
- The Confidence Sequence (strong claim → named limit → reframe as credibility)
- No "ESG" as a self-descriptor — ever
- No "we believe" in formal copy — make the claim directly
- Ethics framed as insight, not constraint
- Every quantitative claim gets a footnote

### 4. Flex tone for context

Use the Tone by Context matrix from the guidelines. Formality, humor, technical depth, and warmth all
shift by context — voice does not.

### 5. Run compliance pre-flight

Before any content is final, check the Compliance Pre-flight Checklist in the guidelines.
Pay special attention to:
- Absolute prohibited terms (strict liability — no intent required)
- Performance claims (require approved phrasing + required disclosures)
- Concentration risk disclosures (required whenever 15–25 holdings are mentioned)
- Media citation format (year required, "Quoted in" not "Endorsed by")

### 6. Explain brand choices

Briefly note which guidelines were applied and why, especially for tone decisions or compliance flags.
If something is a close call, surface it for Sloane to decide.

## Quick reference: ECIC in one sentence

A solo CIO writing like a smart, irreverent friend who happens to be an expert — confident enough to
say "we are wrong, and we accept that," personal enough to sign off with "sending good vibes from Utah."

## Quick reference: three rules

1. Never use "ESG" to describe our own work.
2. Make claims directly — no hedging with "we believe."
3. Acknowledge limits honestly: "We are wrong, and we accept that."
