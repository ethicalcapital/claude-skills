# SEC Marketing Rule — Rule 206(4)-1 Reference

Applied to Utah state-registered RIAs through Utah Administrative Code R164-6-1g(E)(13) and R164-6-1g(E)(6).

## What Counts as an "Advertisement"

**Prong One** (§(e)(1)(i)): Any direct or indirect communication to more than one person (or to any person if it includes hypothetical performance) that offers advisory services to prospective clients or new services to current clients. Websites, social media, newsletters, factsheets, pitch decks, recorded webinars all qualify.

**Prong Two** (§(e)(1)(ii)): Any testimonial or endorsement for which compensation is provided — regardless of audience size.

**Exclusions**: Extemporaneous live oral communications, regulatory-required filings (Form ADV, PPMs), hypothetical performance provided in response to unsolicited one-on-one requests. Communications to retain existing clients without offering new services fall outside the definition but remain subject to anti-fraud provisions.

## Seven General Prohibitions (§(a))

### §(a)(1) — No untrue statements or material omissions
May not include any untrue statement of a material fact, or omit a material fact necessary to make statements not misleading.

### §(a)(2) — No unsubstantiated material claims
Any material statement of fact must have a reasonable basis for substantiation upon SEC demand. Opinions are exempt; factual claims are not. Maintain documentation supporting every factual claim.

### §(a)(3) — No misleading implications
Information that would "reasonably be likely to cause an untrue or misleading implication or inference" about a material fact is prohibited. Example: using a testimonial from someone who hasn't been a client for 20 years without disclosing that fact.

### §(a)(4) — Fair and balanced treatment of benefits and risks
Any discussion of potential benefits must provide "fair and balanced treatment of any material risks or material limitations associated with the potential benefits." **Most frequently violated provision.** Every benefit claim requires a corresponding risk disclosure.

### §(a)(5) — No cherry-picking specific investment advice
References to specific past recommendations must be fair and balanced — successful picks cannot be highlighted without fairly representing unsuccessful ones.

### §(a)(6) — No cherry-picked performance periods
Performance results and time periods must not be included, excluded, or presented in a manner that is not fair and balanced.

### §(a)(7) — Catch-all: no materially misleading advertising
Accurate disclosures in an unreadable font would violate this provision. Standard is principles-based and accounts for audience sophistication.

## Performance Advertising Requirements (§(d))

### Gross vs. net performance (§(d)(1))
**Compliant**: Net-only, or gross + net together with net at equal prominence.
**Violation (automatic HOLD)**: Gross-only — gross may never appear without net.

Net must appear with **at least equal prominence**, in a format facilitating comparison, calculated over the **same time period** using the **same methodology**. Net = after all fees and expenses including advisory fees. If a model fee is used, it must be no higher than the actual fee charged.

### Standardized time periods (§(d)(2))
Performance must show **1-year, 5-year, and 10-year periods** with equal prominence, ending no less recently than the most recent calendar year-end. If the portfolio hasn't existed for a prescribed period, substitute the life of the portfolio. Update within ~one month after year-end.

### No SEC approval claims (§(d)(3))
No statement, express or implied, that performance calculations have been approved or reviewed by the SEC.

### Related performance (§(d)(4))
When showing performance of related portfolios (substantially similar investment policies, objectives, strategies), must include **all related portfolios** unless exclusion would not result in materially higher performance and would not alter required time periods.

### Extracted performance (§(d)(5))
Performance of a subset of investments extracted from a portfolio may not be shown unless the advertisement provides or offers to promptly provide the **total portfolio's performance**. Per SEC March 2025 FAQ: gross extracted performance is permissible if clearly identified as gross, accompanied by total portfolio gross and net with equal prominence, and the total portfolio's period includes the entire extracted performance period.

### Hypothetical performance (§(d)(6))
Includes model portfolio performance, backtested returns, targeted/projected returns. Three conditions must all be met:
1. Adviser must adopt policies ensuring hypothetical performance is **relevant to the likely financial situation and investment objectives of the intended audience** (effectively prohibits mass-market distribution)
2. **Sufficient information** about the criteria and assumptions used in calculating the performance
3. **Sufficient information** about risks and limitations

Hypothetical performance is exempt from 1/5/10-year and related performance requirements.

### Predecessor performance (§(d)(7))
Four conditions: persons primarily responsible for prior results must manage accounts at the advertising adviser; accounts must be sufficiently similar; all similar accounts must be included; clear, prominent disclosure that results were achieved at another entity. Adviser must maintain all original records.

## Testimonials and Endorsements (§(b))

**Testimonial** (§(e)(17)): Statement by a current client about their experience or that solicits/refers clients.
**Endorsement** (§(e)(5)): Statement by a non-client indicating approval or recommendation, or that solicits/refers clients.
**Promoter**: Person giving a compensated testimonial or endorsement.

### Required clear and prominent disclosures (§(b)(1))
- Whether the person is a **current client or not**
- Whether **cash or non-cash compensation** was provided
- Brief statement of any **material conflicts of interest**

Additional disclosures (need not be clear and prominent): material terms of compensation, description of material conflicts from compensation arrangement.

### Oversight (§(b)(2))
Adviser must have a **reasonable basis for believing** the testimonial/endorsement complies with the rule. **Written agreement** required for any compensated promoter describing scope of activities and compensation terms.

### Bad actor disqualification (§(b)(3))
May not compensate any "ineligible person" — anyone subject to a disqualifying SEC action within the prior 10 years.

### De minimis exemption (§(b)(4)(i))
Total compensation ≤ **$1,000 in preceding 12 months** — written agreement and bad actor disqualification don't apply (disclosure and oversight still do).

### Affiliate exemption (§(b)(4)(ii))
Testimonials by the adviser's partners, officers, directors, or employees exempt from disclosure and written agreement requirements, provided affiliation is readily apparent or disclosed.

**Key enforcement finding** (SEC Risk Alert, Dec 16, 2025): Hyperlinked disclosures **fail** the "clear and prominent" standard. Disclosures must be at least as prominent as the testimonial itself.

## Third-Party Ratings (§(c))

Required: (1) reasonable basis for believing any underlying questionnaire was structured to make it equally easy to provide favorable and unfavorable responses and was not designed to produce a predetermined result; (2) clear and prominent disclosure of the date of the rating, time period it covers, identity of the rating entity, and whether compensation was provided.

## Annual Compliance Review (Rule 206(4)-7(b))

The firm must conduct an **annual review** of its compliance policies and procedures. For marketing, this means every piece of active marketing material must be reviewed at least once per calendar year. Any material that cannot be confirmed as reviewed within the past 12 months should be treated as non-compliant until reviewed — this is an automatic HOLD trigger.

**Marketing-specific annual review checklist**:
- [ ] All website content and disclaimers — current and accurate
- [ ] All social media accounts and content
- [ ] Every marketing material in active use (factsheets, pitch decks, newsletters, email templates)
- [ ] Testimonial/endorsement compliance (if used) — verify ongoing Utah Division status
- [ ] Third-party rating compliance — confirm ratings are still current and disclosures accurate
- [ ] Performance advertising accuracy and supporting documentation up to date
- [ ] Books and records completeness — all materials archived, 5-year retention verified
- [ ] Form ADV accuracy regarding marketing practices (Item 5.L)
- [ ] Archiving system effectiveness for social media and electronic communications
- [ ] Staff training adequacy on Marketing Rule requirements

**Timing**: Annual review must be completed. Best practice is to tie it to the Form ADV annual amendment filing (within 90 days of fiscal year end).
