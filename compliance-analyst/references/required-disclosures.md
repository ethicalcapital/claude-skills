# Required Disclosures — Quick Reference

## Mandatory Language by Context

| Context | Required language | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Any use of "registered" | "Registration does not imply a certain level of skill or training" | Form ADV Part 2A, Item 1.C |
| Any performance display | Net and gross with equal prominence; 1/5/10-year periods | Rule 206(4)-1(d)(1)-(2) |
| Any performance display | "Past performance is not indicative of future results" | General prohibition §(a)(4) |
| Any testimonial | Client status, compensation disclosure, conflicts | Rule 206(4)-1(b)(1) |
| Any third-party rating | Date, time period, rating entity, compensation | Rule 206(4)-1(c)(2) |
| Any hypothetical performance | Criteria, assumptions, risks, limitations | Rule 206(4)-1(d)(6) |
| Any predecessor performance | "Results were achieved at another entity" + full disclosures | Rule 206(4)-1(d)(7) |
| Any extracted performance | Total portfolio performance provided or offered | Rule 206(4)-1(d)(5) |
| Any benefits claim | Corresponding material risks and limitations | Rule 206(4)-1(a)(4) |
| Guarantees of outcomes | **Strictly prohibited** | R164-6-1g(E)(12) |
| Email newsletters | Physical address, opt-out mechanism, accurate headers | CAN-SPAM Act |

## Website — Mandatory Disclaimers

**Registration status:**
> "[Firm Name] is a registered investment adviser with the Utah Division of Securities. Registration does not imply a certain level of skill or training."

Also state which states the firm is permitted to conduct business in.

**No SEC/state verification:**
> "The information on this website has not been approved or verified by the United States Securities and Exchange Commission or by any state securities authority."

**No guarantee:**
> "Investing involves risk, including possible loss of principal. No investment strategy or risk management technique can guarantee returns or eliminate risk."

**Past performance (whenever performance is shown):**
> "Past performance is not indicative of future results."

**No client relationship:**
> "Viewing this website does not create an adviser-client relationship. An adviser-client relationship is established only through execution of a written advisory agreement."

**Educational purpose:**
> "The information provided on this website is for educational and informational purposes only and does not constitute investment advice, a recommendation, or an offer to buy or sell securities."

**Website footer must include**: firm legal name, "Registered Investment Adviser" with state(s) of registration, link to Form ADV Part 2A, link to Privacy Policy, physical address and contact information, copyright notice.

## Form ADV Part 2A Cover Page — Mandatory Language

> "This brochure provides information about the qualifications and business practices of [Firm Name]. If you have any questions about the contents of this brochure, please contact us at [telephone/email]. The information in this brochure has not been approved or verified by the United States Securities and Exchange Commission or by any state securities authority."

> "Registration of an investment adviser does not imply any level of skill or training."

**IAPD availability disclosure** (required on brochure cover page):

> "Additional information about [Firm Name] is available on the SEC's Investment Adviser Public Disclosure website at www.adviserinfo.sec.gov."

All Part 2A filings are publicly available through IAPD regardless of whether the firm is SEC- or state-registered.

## Social Media Compliance

All business-related social media is subject to the same advertising rules — the Marketing Rule is **platform neutral**.

**Archiving requirement (Rule 204-2(a)(7) and (a)(11))**: All business-related social media posts, comments, and direct messages must be retained for **5 years from the end of the fiscal year** in which they were last disseminated, with the **first 2 years in an appropriate office**. Third-party archiving solutions are commonly used (Smarsh, PageFreezer, Global Relay).

**Written social media policy required**: Must cover approved platforms, content standards, pre-approval or after-the-fact review procedures, third-party content policies, and training requirements.

**Platform-specific issues**:
- LinkedIn "Recommendations" may constitute testimonials requiring full Marketing Rule disclosures
- Retweets/shares of third-party content may create liability under adoption (endorsing content) or entanglement (involvement in content preparation) theories
- Character-limited platforms (X/Twitter) should link to a full disclosure page
- Form ADV Part 1A must disclose all social media platforms used for business

## Newsletter Compliance

Newsletters are advertisements under the Marketing Rule. Must comply with all seven general prohibitions, include all applicable disclaimers, and present benefits alongside risks.

**CAN-SPAM Act (15 U.S.C. §7701) additionally requires**:
- Accurate header and subject line information
- Valid physical postal address
- Clear opt-out mechanism honored within **10 business days**
- Opt-out functionality maintained for **30 days** after sending
- Penalties: **$16,000+ per violation per email**

All newsletters must be archived for 5 years.

## Factsheets and Pitch Decks

Both are advertisements under the Marketing Rule.

- Performance must show **gross and net** with equal prominence, in **1/5/10-year standardized periods**, with no cherry-picking
- Benchmark comparisons must describe the index, note it is unmanaged and not directly investable, and disclose limitations
- Strategy-specific **risk disclosures** and **fee disclosures** required
- Case studies showing profitable investments must provide overall strategy performance for the relevant period
