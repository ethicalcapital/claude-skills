# FTC Privacy Rules + Books & Records

## Privacy: FTC, Not SEC

**Regulation S-P does not apply** — it covers only SEC-registered investment advisers. This firm's privacy obligations flow through the **FTC Privacy Rule (16 CFR Part 313)** and **FTC Safeguards Rule (16 CFR Part 314)**, which are harmonized with Reg S-P.

## FTC Privacy Rule (16 CFR Part 313)

**Initial privacy notice (§313.4)**: Provided to each customer "not later than when you establish a customer relationship" (typically when the advisory agreement is signed). Must be clear and conspicuous and accurately reflect privacy policies.

**Annual privacy notice (§313.5)**: Required at least once per 12-month period. However, the **FAST Act exception (2015)** eliminates the annual notice requirement if the firm:
1. Only shares nonpublic personal information (NPI) with nonaffiliated third parties under permitted exceptions (service providers, fraud prevention), AND
2. Has not changed its privacy policies since the last notice.

Most small RIAs that don't share NPI beyond permitted exceptions qualify for this exception.

**Required content (§313.6)**:
- Categories of NPI collected
- Categories of NPI disclosed
- Categories of third parties receiving NPI
- Categories of NPI about former customers disclosed
- Opt-out rights and procedures (if applicable)
- Policies for protecting confidentiality and security of NPI

A **Model Privacy Form** (Appendix A) provides safe-harbor compliance. If the firm does not disclose NPI to nonaffiliated third parties beyond statutory exceptions, a simplified notice is permitted.

**Opt-out requirements (§§313.7, 313.10)**: Required only if NPI is disclosed to nonaffiliated third parties beyond permitted exceptions.

## FTC Safeguards Rule (16 CFR Part 314)

Explicitly covers investment advisers not registered with the SEC. As amended effective **June 9, 2023**, requires:
- Designated **Qualified Individual** to oversee information security
- Written **risk assessment**
- Access controls, data encryption, multi-factor authentication
- Regular monitoring and testing
- Personnel security training
- Written **incident response plan**
- Oversight of service providers

**Small business exception**: Some requirements (written risk assessment, penetration testing, MFA) do not apply to firms maintaining customer information for **fewer than 5,000 consumers** — this firm's 78 clients likely qualify.

**Breach notification (effective May 13, 2024)**: FTC must be notified within 30 days if a notification event involves **500+ consumers**.

## Website Privacy Policy

No single federal law mandates a website privacy policy, but GLBA, FTC rules, and state expectations create a de facto requirement. Must include:
- Categories of personal information collected
- How it is used
- Third parties with whom it is shared
- Data security measures
- Cookie and tracking technology disclosures
- Contact information for privacy inquiries
- Effective date

Accessible via footer link on every page.

## Utah Consumer Privacy Act — Does Not Apply

The UCPA (Utah Code §13-61-101, effective December 31, 2023) provides a broad entity-level exemption for financial institutions governed by GLBA. The firm also falls below the UCPA's thresholds ($25M annual revenue; 100,000+ consumers). Utah's data breach notification statute (§§13-44-101 et seq.) similarly exempts GLBA financial institutions.

## Books and Records (Rule 204-2)

**What must be retained**:
- Copy of every advertisement disseminated (all formats — print, digital, social media, email)
- Written or recorded materials used with oral advertisements
- Disclosure records for compensated testimonials/endorsements
- Copies of questionnaires/surveys underlying third-party ratings used
- Documentation substantiating reasonable basis for believing testimonials, endorsements, and ratings comply
- Record of names of all affiliates providing testimonials under the affiliate exemption
- All performance calculation backup — account statements, worksheets, internal working papers
- All hypothetical performance materials provided or offered to intended audiences
- All written communications about predecessor performance
- All electronic communications (emails, texts, social media DMs) related to business

**Retention period**: 5 years from end of fiscal year during which the advertisement was last disseminated, with the **first 2 years in an appropriate office**. Must be maintained in an easily accessible format.
