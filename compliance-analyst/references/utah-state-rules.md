# Utah State Rules Reference

## Regulatory Framework

- **Utah Uniform Securities Act**: Title 61, Chapter 1
- **Utah Administrative Code**: R164
- **Critical advertising provision**: R164-6-1g (defines "dishonest or unethical business practices")

## Key Subsections of R164-6-1g

### §(E)(13) — SEC Marketing Rule Incorporation
"Publishing, circulating or distributing any advertisement which does not comply with Rule 206(4)-1 under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940."
→ Directly incorporates the full SEC Marketing Rule as Utah's advertising baseline.

### §(E)(6) — Federal Rules Apply Regardless of Registration Status
Federal provisions referenced in §(E) apply to investment advisers "regardless of whether the federal provision limits its application to advisers subject to federal registration."
→ Confirms the SEC Marketing Rule applies to this Utah state-registered firm.

### §(E)(8) — No Misrepresentation of Qualifications or Services
Prohibits misrepresenting qualifications, nature of services, or fees to any client or prospective client.

### §(E)(11) — Disclose All Material Conflicts of Interest
Requires disclosure of all material conflicts of interest.

### §(E)(12) — No Guarantees
Prohibits guaranteeing that a specific result will be achieved — no promises of gains or protection from losses. **Absolute prohibition.**

### §(C)(32) / §(D)(7) — Misleading Use of Certifications or Designations
Prohibits misleading use of professional certifications or designations.

## Anti-Fraud Provisions

**§61-1-1 (Fraud Unlawful)**: Broadly prohibits employing any device, scheme, or artifice to defraud; making untrue statements of material fact; or engaging in acts that operate as fraud in connection with securities.

**§61-1-2**: Extends §61-1-1 specifically to investment advisers. All public-facing marketing materials are subject to these anti-fraud provisions.

## Filing and Review

Utah does **not** require pre-filing or pre-approval of advertisements. However, the Utah Division lists "approving advertisements and seminars" among ongoing compliance responsibilities — **internal review and approval processes are expected**. The Division reviews "all advertisements, including website and performance advertising, for accuracy" during examinations.

## Testimonial Uncertainty — Important

The existing **NASAA model rule** (USA 2002 502(b) Section (o)(1)) prohibits testimonials for state-registered RIAs. However, Utah's R164-6-1g(E)(13) references the current SEC Rule 206(4)-1, which permits testimonials with conditions. NASAA proposed amendments in **July 2025** to align model rules with the SEC Marketing Rule; the comment period closed August 28, 2025.

**Action required**: Confirm directly with the Utah Division of Securities (801-530-6600) whether testimonials are currently permitted under Utah's interpretation before using any testimonials in marketing.

## Form ADV Requirements (State-Registered Additions)

Beyond standard Part 2A items, state-registered advisers must include:
- Formal education and business background of principal officers and management persons
- Description of non-advisory business activities
- Disciplinary events of management persons
- Supervisory chain information

**Form CRS is NOT required** for this firm — applies only to SEC-registered advisers. Utah has not adopted a state Form CRS requirement.

**Delivery requirements**:
- Initial delivery to each client at or before entering the advisory agreement
- Annual updating amendment filed through IARD within **90 days** of fiscal year end
- Summary of material changes delivered to clients within **120 days** of fiscal year end, or full updated brochure
- Interim amendments for disciplinary information must be delivered promptly
